# Understanding Methylation Triage

Cervical cancer screening has evolved significantly over the past decades. While HPV testing has high sensitivity, it lacks specificity, leading to many unnecessary colposcopy referrals. This is where **methylation triage** comes in.

## The Problem with HPV Testing

HPV infections are common, and most clear up on their own. Testing positive for HPV doesn't mean a woman has precancerous lesions. However, current protocols often require invasive follow-ups for HPV-positive women.

## The Solution: PAX1 and JAM3

Our research focuses on two specific genes: **PAX1** and **JAM3**. When these genes become methylated (a chemical modification to the DNA), it is a strong indicator of high-grade lesions (CIN2+).

### Key Advantages

1.  **High Specificity**: Reduces false positives.
2.  **Non-Invasive**: Uses the same sample as the HPV test.
3.  **Objective**: Automated analysis reduces human error.

## Clinical Data

In a multi-center study involving over 10,000 women, the PAX1/JAM3 panel demonstrated a high accuracy for detecting CIN2+.

> "Methylation triage represents the next generation of cervical cancer screening, offering peace of mind to patients and clarity to clinicians."

## Future Outlook

We are currently expanding our research to include endometrial and ovarian cancers, applying the same principles of epigenetic detection.
