## Product Overview

CISPOLY is a DNA methylation-based early detection platform for gynecologic cancers, developed by Beijing Cispoly Biotech. Our product portfolio covers three major cancer types: cervical, endometrial, and ovarian cancer.

### Core Products

| Product | Target Cancer | Core Technology | Clinical Performance |
|---------|--------------|-----------------|---------------------|
| **CISCER** | Cervical Cancer & Precancerous Lesions | PAX1/JAM3 Dual-Gene Methylation | Sensitivity 89.6%, Specificity 96.5% |
| **CISENDO** | Endometrial Cancer | CDO1/CELF4 Dual-Gene Methylation | Sensitivity 94.5%, Specificity 94.2% |
| **CISOVA** | Ovarian Cancer | CDO1/HOXA9 Dual-Gene Methylation | Sensitivity 93.2%, Specificity 92.8% |

---

## Key Advantages

- **High Sensitivity**: Superior sensitivity compared to conventional cytology testing
- **High Specificity**: Reduces unnecessary colposcopy referrals and over-treatment
- **Non-invasive**: Requires only cervical exfoliated cells or vaginal secretion samples
- **Self-Sampling Compatible**: Enables at-home self-collection, improving screening coverage
- **Clinically Validated**: Incorporated into multiple Chinese expert consensus and clinical guidelines

## Clinical Validation

CISPOLY products have completed multi-center clinical trials across **30+** tertiary hospitals in China:

- **CISCER**: 12 centers, 1,968 participants

- **CISOVA**: 16 centers

Leading institutions include: Peking Union Medical College Hospital (Lead), Qilu Hospital of Shandong University, The Second Affiliated Hospital of Zhejiang University School of Medicine, and Obstetrics & Gynecology Hospital of Fudan University.

## Guidelines & Consensus

CISPOLY methylation detection technology has been incorporated into the following authoritative guidelines and expert consensus:

- Expert Consensus on PAX1/JAM3 Dual-Gene Methylation Detection for Cervical Cancer (2025)
- Guideline for Cervical Cancer Screening in China (Part 2) (2025)
- Chinese Expert Consensus on Standardized Selection of Cervical Cancer Gene Testing (2025)
- Chinese Expert Consensus on Tertiary Prevention Strategies for Endometrial Cancer (2025)
- China Cervical Cancer Comprehensive Prevention and Control Blue Book (2023)
